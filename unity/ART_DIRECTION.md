# Art direction — civic surrealism

The Idea Zoo is not a realistic city and not a fantasy theme park. It is a living civic institution built from paper, brass, clay, glass, ink, maintenance pipes and public language.

## World rules

- Architecture reveals who has authority.
- Maintenance is always visible somewhere.
- Every department has a different relationship with uncertainty.
- Useful creatures leave wear marks, schedules and staff routines.
- Dangerous creatures are not automatically ugly.
- Small ideas receive the same visual care as predators.
- Public phrases can become weather, signage, gravity or migration patterns.

## Implemented shape language

Whisper Gate now uses open arches, layered paper walls, intake drawers, queue rails and hanging civic standards.

Hatchery uses circular forms, suspended civic instruments, incubation pipework, vitrines and warm glass.

Behaviour spaces use different evidence props: listening stones and unsaid needs in Desire Yard; tokens, pledge rails and contract furniture in Commitment Paddock; maintenance tags, ledgers and visible pipes in Burrower Tunnel; multiple exit doors and appeal rails at Refusal Gate.

Board Wing uses rigid blocks, barriers, red premature seals and classifications stamped before evidence arrives.

Molt House uses spools, seams, surgical frames and incomplete symmetry.

Decision Garden uses five spatially distinct standards and gates rather than a menu.

## Character language

The Keeper wears a civic field coat, Resonance Lens, containment thread and five ruling plates. There are no weapons.

Six specialist silhouettes now communicate belief before dialogue:

- Mara Rook: hatch fork and protective paper cape.
- Toma Reed: release staff and recall sash.
- Sefu Anik: appetite lens and specimen vials.
- Elian Thread: Molt spool and surgical frame.
- Sen Osei: counterfactual frames and forecast coat.
- Nara Voss: mercy bell and White Room plate.

These procedural rigs establish silhouette, posture and tool language. Imported skeletal meshes can replace them later without changing roles or staging.

## Creature language

Procedural assembly chooses from authored presentation parts. Appetite affects mouth, eyes, collar, harness, tokens, trust nodes or clock spines. Hidden burden appears as carried weight and tethers. Evidence makes the form more defined rather than merely larger. Guardrails appear as visible rings after a Molt.

Each class has its own strong phone-scale silhouette:

- Fleck: paper wings and gentle light.
- Hand: working harness and task plates.
- Mirror: glass antlers and reflection shards.
- Teeth: civic teeth and crown frame.
- Swarm: many orbiting choir bodies.
- Weather: layered pressure ribbons.
- Burrower: archive plates and maintenance spine.

## Cinematic and sound language

Hatching, inspection, Molt, Decision and ruling use short PlayableDirector camera beats with restrained cinematic bars. The sequence falls back safely when Cinemachine runtime APIs differ.

Sound uses a low civic hum, paper-like noise, restrained bells and stage-specific chords. It should feel institutional and alive, not heroic or militaristic.

## Mobile budget

Prefer strong silhouettes, shared materials and controlled translucency. Small props and environmental text cull by distance. Device tiers adjust shadows, anti-aliasing, LOD bias and render scale. Detail should come from composition, decals, props, animation and sound—not excessive geometry or tiny unreadable texture detail.
