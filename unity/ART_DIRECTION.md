# Art direction — civic surrealism

The Idea Zoo is not a realistic city and not a fantasy theme park. It is a living civic institution built from paper, brass, clay, glass, ink, maintenance pipes and public language.

## World rules

- Architecture reveals who has authority.
- Maintenance is always visible somewhere.
- Every department has a different relationship with uncertainty.
- Useful creatures leave wear marks, schedules and staff routines.
- Dangerous creatures are not automatically ugly.
- Small ideas receive the same visual care as predators.
- Public phrases can become weather, signage, gravity or migration patterns.

## Shape language

Whisper Gate uses open arches, layered paper and translucent glass.

Hatchery uses circular forms, suspended pods and warm civic instruments.

Behaviour spaces use modular test furniture that visibly changes incentives.

Board Wing uses rigid blocks, sealed windows and classifications stamped before evidence arrives.

Molt House uses spools, seams, surgical frames and incomplete symmetry.

Decision Garden uses five spatially distinct gates rather than a menu.

## Character language

The Keeper wears a civic field coat, Resonance Lens, containment thread and five ruling plates. There are no weapons.

Staff silhouettes must remain readable at phone scale. Each specialist needs one tool, one posture and one workspace that communicates their belief before they speak.

## Creature language

Procedural assembly chooses from authored parts. Appetite affects mouth, eyes and feeding motion. Hidden burden appears as carried weight, tethered workers, maintenance growth or exhausted support organisms. Evidence makes the form more defined, not merely larger.

## Mobile budget

Prefer strong silhouettes, baked lighting, shared materials and controlled translucency. Detail should come from composition, decals, props, animation and sound—not excessive geometry or tiny unreadable texture detail.
